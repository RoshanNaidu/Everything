It all started with a question, Radhika. The password for Wi-Fi. You did not even realise that you had already hacked my heart.

Throughout all this time, I tried to ignore every expression of affection that kept getting built inside my mind whenever I saw you. I kept thinking that emotions and feelings might hinder the future I wanted to build, so I tried to stay quiet.

Eventually, I decided that I would confess what I had been carrying, but I feared it might sabotage our friendship, and that friendship is something I value more than I can ever properly explain to you.

At the end of August 2025, before leaving for the US, I wanted to tell you everything. How you arrived in my dreams, how you appeared in the little things around me through the day and night like the headlamps, the moon, and every small thing that somehow reminded me of you.

On 12 August, I carefully hinted that I would feel better if you came to meet me. Unfortunately, it did not happen. I came to the US, and every single day I kept thinking about coming back in summer for my birthday week, when we would definitely meet.

15 June 2026 was already planned in my mind on 14 February 2026. That was the day I wanted to confess. But again, I felt an online confession would never have the same impact, so I decided to wait. Again.

And then, look at how dangerously messed up my luck was: the exact moment I had been waiting for, for almost one year, was the exact one week you got sick.

I had imagined everything -> playing at the gaming lounge in the afternoon, going to the most aesthetic cafés and restaurants in the evening, eating the most aesthetic ice cream at night and bringing you the bouquet I had ordered, and making you feel the happiest before finally letting you know what I feel.

On the 19th, when we met and exchanged gifts, I went home and had tears in my eyes for the first time because of how I felt, since the time Sheero had left me for heaven.

Since that day, it has been so difficult for me, thinking that it may take an eternity to meet you again, especially when you say you might leave India by the time I come back for a while.
